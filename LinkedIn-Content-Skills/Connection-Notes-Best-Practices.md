# LinkedIn Connection Notes - Best Practices

> **Purpose**: Guidelines for writing effective, non-generic LinkedIn connection notes that actually get accepted and start real conversations.

**Last Updated**: 2026-01-27
**Use Case**: Writing connection requests to experts, founders, potential partners

---

## Core Philosophy

Connection notes should be **founder-to-founder**, not salesperson-to-prospect. You're a smart person reaching out to another smart person whose work you genuinely follow.

---

## ❌ What NEVER Works (Generic Patterns)

### Generic Openers
- ❌ "I'd love to connect"
- ❌ "Would love to learn from you"
- ❌ "I'm curious about..."
- ❌ "I appreciate your work on..."
- ❌ "Your background is impressive"

### Generic Structure
- ❌ Starting with "I" or talking about yourself first
- ❌ "Your work aligns with what we're building" (makes it about you)
- ❌ Any questions in the first message
- ❌ Mentioning your product/company in detail
- ❌ Using words like "curious", "fascinating", "spot on"

### Why These Fail
They sound AI-generated or templated. Everyone sees through them immediately.

---

## ✅ What Actually Works

### 1. Start With Specific Content You Saw

**Good examples:**
- "Your Hightide 120-day experiment caught my eye"
- "The org chart you posted showing your 40+ AI agents"
- "Your posts on modular video ad frameworks keep showing up in my feed"
- "Your Sovran modular video approach keeps showing up in my feed"

**Why it works:** Shows you actually follow their work, not just found them via search.

---

### 2. Show You Understand What Makes It Interesting

Don't just praise — show *why* it caught your attention:

**Good examples:**
- "That's serious consistency in a space where most podcasts fade after 50"
- "That's the kind of test most people theorize about but never actually run"
- "The way you're automating creative remixing for Meta — that's the bottleneck most performance teams can't solve"
- "Proves ad performance isn't the black box everyone pretends it is"
- "The kind of systematic thinking most creative teams skip"

**Why it works:** Demonstrates domain expertise and genuine understanding, not surface-level praise.

---

### 3. Personal Reaction (Not Generic Praise)

**Good:**
- "That framing clicked for me more than most AI content I've seen"
- "Just wanted to say — respect the work"
- "Would enjoy connecting sometime"

**Bad:**
- "Great post!"
- "Love what you're doing"
- "Very insightful"

---

### 4. Minimal or No Ask

**Best options (in order of preference):**

1. **No closer at all** — just the observation about their work + your name
   - End with the insight/observation, then just "Seva"

2. **Respect/appreciation**
   - "Respect the work."
   - "Just wanted to say — respect the work."

3. **Super light interest**
   - "Would enjoy connecting sometime."

**Never:**
- "Building AI agents for..." (sounds like LinkedIn headline)
- Ask questions
- Request a call/meeting
- Pitch your product
- Ask for their opinion on something

---

## Formula

```
[Specific content you saw] + [Why it's interesting/what it solves] + [Optional: ultra-light closer]
```

**Length**: 2-4 sentences maximum (under 300 characters ideal for LinkedIn)

- Shows understanding of what makes it hard

## What To Mention About Yourself (If At All)

**CRITICAL RULE: Don't mention yourself at all in connection notes.**

Connection notes should be 100% about their work, not about you.

**Why "Building X" sounds bad:**
- "Building AI agents for performance marketing. Seva" → sounds like LinkedIn headline, not human
- Creates weird disconnect between statement and name
- Too formal, too resume-like
- Makes it about you, not them

**If you absolutely must mention context:**

**Least bad options (use sparingly):**
- "I'm working on AI agents for this exact problem." (integrates naturally)
- "We're tackling the same problem with AI agents." (creates connection)

**Never use:**
- "Building AI agents for performance marketing." (LinkedIn headline)
- "Working on AI agents for ad optimization." (resume line)
- "I'm building tools for..." (sounds like selling)
- "We build optimization tools that help..." (pitch)
- Any standalone statement about what you do

**Best approach:** Don't mention yourself at all. The connection note is about THEM and their work. Save your context for after they accept or for Touch 1 message.

## When To Send

- **Best days**: Tuesday-Thursday, mornings (9-11am their timezone)
- **Avoid**: Monday mornings, Friday afternoons, weekends
- **After engaging**: Only send after you've liked/commented on 2-3 of their posts first

## Template for Quick Reference

```
Hi [Name],

[Specific piece of content]. [Why it caught your attention / what problem it solves / why it's hard].

[Optional ultra-light closer or nothing]

[Your Name]
```

---

## Red Flags - Edit If You See These

If your note includes ANY of these, rewrite:
- ❌ "I noticed..."
- ❌ "I'm curious..."
- ❌ "I appreciate..."
- ❌ "Would love to learn..."
- ❌ "Your experience in..."
- ❌ "I think we could..."
- ❌ Any questions
- ❌ More than 4 sentences
- ❌ Detailed description of your company
- ❌ Use of words: "fascinating", "impressed", "intrigued", "aligned"

---

## Real-World Test

Before sending, ask yourself:
1. Could this note have been sent to 50 other people with minor name changes? → **Rewrite**
2. Did I mention specific content I actually saw? → **If no, rewrite**
3. Does it sound like I'm selling something? → **If yes, rewrite**
4. Would I accept this connection request? → **If no, rewrite**
