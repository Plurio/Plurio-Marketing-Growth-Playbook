# LinkedIn Articles Plan — Template

> **Purpose:** Plan a series of LinkedIn Articles optimized for both AI-search (GEO / Generative Engine Optimization) and the LinkedIn algorithm (360Brew).
>
> **Framework:** GEO Guide + Hook Bank + Algorithm Intelligence — three sources, see paired docs.
>
> **Target:** Articles that get cited by ChatGPT / Perplexity / Google AI Overview when a user asks about your niche.

---

## Why Articles (not just posts)

- **50–66% of all AI-cited LinkedIn content = Articles** (not short posts). Articles are longer, more structured, and index better.
- Posts are still critical for engagement and the LinkedIn algorithm — but Articles win on AI citations.
- Standard cadence: **1 Article/week + 2–3 posts** in the same week.

## Publishing Schedule (for a series of 3+ articles)

| Week | Article | Companion Post |
|------|---------|----------------|
| Week 1 | Article 1: [Topic] | Post: teaser hook → link to Article in the first comment |
| Week 2 | Article 2: [Topic] | Post: snippet from the article + question |
| Week 3 | Article 3: [Topic] | Post: framework callback + Article link |

**Timing:** Publish Articles at your audience's peak activity (usually Tuesday 6–7 AM in the audience's timezone). Companion post — Wednesday or Thursday of the same week.

**Company Page cross-post:** publish every Article **on the Company Page as well**. Perplexity cites Company Pages in 59% of cases (higher than personal profiles in some niches).

---

## GEO Checklist for each Article

Before publishing:

- [ ] Title is phrased as a ChatGPT/Perplexity query
- [ ] First paragraph **directly answers** the question in the title (2–3 sentences)
- [ ] H2 subheadings, short paragraphs, logical flow
- [ ] Each section **works standalone** (an LLM can extract a single section)
- [ ] Profession-specific terminology in the target audience's language
- [ ] Contains **unique data**: own benchmarks, cases, research, named concepts
- [ ] 500–2,000 words
- [ ] 95% original content
- [ ] Keywords match the profile headline + recent post topics (Profile-Content Alignment)
- [ ] Published on BOTH personal profile AND Company Page
- [ ] Direct URLs saved for downstream citation monitoring

## What to measure after publication

See [`../Knowledge-Base/10-Articles-and-GEO/LLM-Visibility-Audit-Methodology.md`](../Knowledge-Base/10-Articles-and-GEO/LLM-Visibility-Audit-Methodology.md) — the full audit methodology.

Bare minimum, 4 weeks after publication:

1. **Direct URL discoverability** — is the article URL found via `site:linkedin.com/pulse` queries
2. **Content citation in AI summaries** — do Google AI Overview / Perplexity quote phrasing from the article
3. **Topic-specific queries** — for your unique concepts (named frameworks), does your article come up in results

## What to include and NOT include

| Include | Don't include |
|---------|---------------|
| Unique concepts with their own names ("trust adoption curve", "5 levels of X") | Generic phrases with no attribution potential |
| Concrete numbers with context ($X spent, N% improvement) | Smooth-talker phrases without data |
| Own benchmarks / proprietary data | Repurposed data from other people's reports with no new angle |
| Real cases (with client permission) | NDA cases without sign-off |
| Links to own resources (product docs, open-source repos) | External links with no backlink strategy |
| Co-authored content (when there's a co-author with their own posts) | One-author content where the team could be cross-linked |
