## ⛔ CANONICAL SOURCE RULE — CRITICALLY IMPORTANT FOR ALL CONTENT UNITS

> This rule applies to ALL content units: DIP profiles, LinkedIn posts, PR comments, event promos, product blurbs, articles.

### What counts as SOURCE (INPUT):
- Transcripts of meetings, calls, webinars, podcasts, conferences
- Recordings of talks and interviews
- Telegram posts written by the person themselves
- Voice input and direct text from the author's head
- Transcripts of client testimonials
- Documents, articles, CVs personally written by the author

### What counts as OUTPUT (NEVER used as INPUT):
- LinkedIn posts from `Posted/` — these are the PRODUCT of the system, not a source
- PR comments — repackaged content
- Marketing copy, product blurbs — marketing text
- Decks and slides — edited content
- Any AI-generated content

### Why this matters:
**DIP → Post → DIP = Feedback Loop.** Within 3-4 cycles the person's real voice is lost. AI starts feeding on its own output, and the content becomes generic.

### The rule:
1. **DIP is built ONLY from raw sources** (see `_DIP-Creation-Workflow.md`, `_DIP-Update-Workflow.md`)
2. **Posts are created from DIP + raw sources**, but the posts themselves NEVER become a source for DIP or other posts
3. **Published content is scanned ONLY for:** performance analysis, avoiding repetition, understanding what has already been covered
4. **When creating any content** — always look for raw materials (transcripts, voice input), do not recycle output

## PART 1: THE PROFILE — THE FOUNDATION OF EVERYTHING

> Sources: Rock Your LinkedIn Profile (Rock Your LinkedIn Profile course), Optimizing Your LinkedIn Profile Nano Tips (Profile Nano Tips course), How to Build a Strong Professional Brand (How to Build a Strong Brand course), How to Stand Out on LinkedIn (How to Stand Out course), Marketing on LinkedIn (Marketing on LinkedIn course), 360Brew Algorithm Architecture

### 1.1 Why the profile is 50% of success

360Brew **cross-checks** post content against the profile (Headline, About, Experience). **Semantic divergence** between what you write and how your profile is described → **distribution suppression**.

| Profile element | Algorithmic role | What to do |
|----------------|------------------------|------------|
| **Headline** | Anchor for topic classification. 360Brew matches the headline against post topics | Should contain 2-3 core topics you write about |
| **About / Summary** | Extended semantic signal for the algorithm | Storytelling + industry keywords. Not a CV, a narrative |
| **Current Role** | Mandatory field. Empty = semantic gap | Always filled, even for a founder/freelancer |
| **Experience** | Confirmation of expertise depth | Career trajectory told as a story |
| **Skills & Endorsements** | Search signal + credibility | Top 3 skills = what you want to be found for |
| **Featured Section** | The first thing a visitor sees after the headline | Best posts, articles, links to key resources |
| **Recommendations** | Social proof | Ask clients and partners, reuse in other sections |

### 1.2 Headline: the formula

**Formula:** `[Role/Identity] | [Core Expertise 1] + [Core Expertise 2] | [Unique Differentiator]`

**Examples:**
- Seva: `CEO @ Plurio | Building AI agents that run marketing | 20+ years in performance marketing, now automating it`
- Kirill: `Head of Growth @ Plurio | AI Agents for Performance Marketing | Turning data chaos into automated decisions`
- Plurio Page tagline: `AI agents for performance marketing teams | From data chaos to automated decisions`

**Rules:**
- Max 220 characters (LinkedIn limit), but only the first 60 are visible in the feed
- Keywords from post topics MUST be in the headline
- Avoid buzzwords without substance ("Thought Leader", "Visionary", "Guru")
- Update when content focus shifts

### 1.3 About / Summary: structure

**Formula (from LinkedIn Learning + our adaptation):**

```
Paragraph 1: HOOK — Who you are and why it matters (2-3 sentences)
  → Not "I am a passionate..." but specifics: "I've spent 20 years..."

Paragraph 2: STORY — Key turning point / transformation
  → "After building X, I realized Y. That's why I'm now doing Z."

Paragraph 3: WHAT I DO NOW — Current focus with specifics
  → "At Plurio, we're building AI agents that..."

Paragraph 4: PROOF — Numbers, cases, social proof
  → "100+ clients. $15M agency built. 30+ person AI-first team."

Paragraph 5: CTA — What to do next
  → "DM me if [specific reason]. I share [topic] here weekly."
```

**Important:**
- Write in first person (I/my)
- Industry keywords should appear naturally in the text
- Format: short paragraphs with whitespace (easy to scan)
- Update every 3-6 months

### 1.6 Recommendations (from Rock Your LinkedIn Profile, Tier 2)

**Why it matters:** Recommendations = social proof from real people. One of the strongest trust signals on the profile.

**How to get quality recommendations:**
1. **Choose the right people** — colleagues, managers, clients, partners who saw the impact firsthand
2. **Personalize the request** — not LinkedIn's generic template, but specifics: "Would you highlight our work on [project] and the [specific result]?"
3. **Pro tip for busy executives:** Draft the recommendation yourself → "Feel free to tweak it to match your voice and style" (Rock Your LinkedIn Profile course, Tier 2). Busy leaders are more likely to write it if given a starting point
4. **Minimum 3 recent** recommendations on the profile
5. You can reuse quotes from recommendations in other sections

### 1.7 Custom URL & Verifications

- **Custom URL:** linkedin.com/in/firstname-lastname (clean, for business cards and resumes)
- **Verifications:** Enable workplace verification, email verification → trust signal. **Verified profiles get 60% more profile views** (LinkedIn data, Rock Your LinkedIn Profile)
  - Identity verification: government-issued ID (limited by geography)
  - Workplace verification: work email, Microsoft Entra Verified ID, LinkedIn Recruiter license
  - **Free.** Can be removed at any time
- **Website link:** Add the company website directly under the headline
- **Name pronunciation:** Add audio — a personal touch for connections

### 2.6 Copywriting for LinkedIn

**From the courses: Marketing on LinkedIn (Marketing on LinkedIn course), Create LinkedIn Posts that Stand Out (Posts that Stand Out course)**

**Copywriting rules:**

| Rule | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **First 210 chars = everything** | Only this is visible before "See more". 60-70% drop off here. The hook MUST live here |
| **"Lost-in-Distance" Effect** | 360Brew gives the first sentence disproportionate attention weighting. A generic prologue → deprioritization BEFORE people scroll through |
| **One post = one idea** | Multiple ideas = confusion. Split them into a series |
| **Short paragraphs** | 1-3 lines. Whitespace between paragraphs. 360Brew parses structured text better |
| **Specifics > abstraction** | "We cut creative testing costs by $3.45 per creative" > "We reduced costs significantly" |
| **First person** | I/my in personal posts. NEVER we/our |
| **Natural keywords** | Industry keywords in the body — more important than hashtags for 360Brew |
| **CTA** | Specific: "Save this for your next campaign review" > generic "What do you think?" |

**Copywriting anti-patterns:**

| Do NOT | Why | Instead |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| "I wanted to share..." | Weak hook, zero curiosity | Start with a specific statement |
| "In today's world..." | Generic, AI-sounding | A concrete observation |
| "Excited to announce..." | Overused LinkedIn cliché | Lead with the VALUE of the announcement |
| "I'm so humbled by..." | Transparently false humility | Be direct about the achievement |
| "Agree?" at the end | Engagement bait, penalized | A concrete question on the topic |

## PART 3: THE 360BREW ALGORITHM — HOW LINKEDIN RANKS CONTENT

> Sources: Algorithm-Intelligence.md (our comprehensive research), LinkedIn Engineering Blog March 2026, Richard van der Blom 1.8M posts, Buffer 2M+ posts, AuthoredUp 621K posts, Hootsuite, Sprout Social

### 3.1 360Brew architecture (March 2026)

**360Brew** is a 150B-parameter decoder-only transformer foundation model. Two stages:

1. **Stage 1 — Unified Retrieval (LLaMA 3 fine-tune):** A single LLM for all feed candidates. Dual-encoder: embeddings for both users AND content. Updates in minutes. Retrieval under 50ms. **Links topics across different terminology.**

2. **Stage 2 — Generative Recommender Ranking:** Causal attention — user history as an ordered sequence. Captures the evolution of interests. H100 GPU clusters.

**Fundamental shift:**

| Old algorithm | 360Brew (2026) |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Summing numeric features | Probabilistic semantic reasoning |
| Hashtags, formatting, timing = levers | Understanding language, context, intent |
| Signals could be "hacked" | The AI reads content like a human |
| One model per task | One foundation model for 30+ tasks |

**Takeaway:** The era of "sending signals" is ending. Quality of thought > technical tricks.

### 3.2 Eight ranking factors

| # | Factor | What to do |
|---|--------|-----------|
| 1 | **Profile-Content Alignment** | Headline + About match post topics. Current Role is required |
| 2 | **Topic Consistency** | ~90 days of consistent posting for semantic categorization. 3-4 pillars max |
| 3 | **First Sentence = Anchor** | The first line = disproportionate attention weighting. Specific, not generic |
| 4 | **Saves > Likes** | 1 save = 5-10x a like. Frameworks, templates, guides = save machines |
| 5 | **Comments Train Profile** | What you comment on → trains the model. Comments from industry peers carry more weight |
| 6 | **Links = Heavy Penalty** | ~60% reach reduction. "Link in first comment" is also penalized (2026) |
| 7 | **Structure Helps AI** | Clear paragraphs improve parsing. Natural keywords > hashtags |
| 8 | **Hashtags = Minimal** | 1-3 max. More = spam-flag risk. 360Brew does not need hashtags |

### 3.3 Engagement Signals: hierarchy by weight

| Signal | Weight | What it means |
|--------|-----|---------------|
| **Saves** | Highest (1 = 5-10x a like) | Content people return to |
| **DM Shares/Sends** | Very High | Private shares = a strong signal |
| **Reposts** | Very High | Especially from influential users |
| **Comments 15+ words** | Very High (2.5x) | 50 comments > 500 likes for reach (3x) |
| **Comment Thread Depth** | High (NEW) | Back-and-forth discussions |
| **Dwell Time** | High | 31-60 seconds = max distribution |
| **Comments (any)** | High (2x vs likes) | Velocity in the first 60-120 min |
| **Click-through ("See more")** | Medium-High | Interest in long content |
| **Profile visits** | Medium | Genuine interest in the author |
| **Reactions (likes)** | Baseline (min weight) | The lowest weight |

### 3.4 Content Lifecycle: 4 stages

```
STAGE 1: Quality Check (0-60 min)
  └─ Shown to 2-5% of the network. Initial assessment.

STAGE 2: Golden Window (0-2 hours) ← DETERMINES 70% OF REACH
  └─ High engagement → expansion into interest-based clusters (60% of reach)
  └─ Low → the post "dies"

STAGE 3: 8-Hour Review
  └─ Sustained engagement. Are the comments still flowing?

STAGE 4: Extended Distribution (24-48 hours)
  └─ Broader distribution. Quality posts live for 48+ hours.
  └─ LinkedIn deliberately surfaces older posts above fresh ones.
```

**Golden Window Protocol (first 2 hours):**

| Action | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Reply to ALL comments in the first 30 min | +64% total comments, 2.3x views |
| Reply in the first 15 min | +90% algorithmic boost |
| Do NOT edit the post | Editing resets the algorithm |
| Comment on 5-10 posts in the niche right after | +20% reach, +55% profile views |
| Warn the boosters in advance | Early engagement from relevant people |

### 3.5 Distribution: how it works in 2026

**Order:**
1. Active relationship clusters (1st-degree, interactive)
2. Interest-based clusters (**60% of reach** goes here)
3. Non-connected users interested in the topic

**Zero-shot reasoning** in 360Brew matches niche authors to audiences based on semantic depth, not network size.

**What this means:** Network size matters less than content depth. Niche expertise = an advantage, not a constraint.

## PART 4: ENGAGEMENT — GROWTH THROUGH INTERACTION

> Sources: Comments-Engine.md, CMO's Guide to Engaging on LinkedIn, Executive's Guide to Engaging on LinkedIn, Essential Skills for Social Media Managers (Brian Honigman), Algorithm-Intelligence.md

### 4.1 Comments as a growth tool

Comments are NOT secondary content. Every comment is a micro-post that:
- Trains the algorithm on your expertise areas (Comments Train Profile)
- Gives +20% reach to your own posts
- Gives +55% profile views
- Builds relationships with key people in the niche

**Daily commenting rhythm:**

| When | What | How much |
|-------|-----|--------|
| **Right after publishing a post** | Comment on 5-10 posts in your niche | 15-30 min |
| **Throughout the day** | Reply to comments on your own posts | As they come in |
| **Evening** | Comment on another 3-5 posts from key connections | 10-15 min |

### 4.2 Comment quality

| A good comment | A bad comment |
|--------------------|--------------------|
| 15+ words, adds perspective | "Great post!" |
| Contains specifics from experience | "Love this!" |
| Asks a smart follow-up question | "So true!" |
| Advances the discussion | "Thanks for sharing!" |
| Written in the author's tone of voice | Generic approval |

**"Great post!" = noise.** The algorithm actively downranks low-entropy engagement. It evaluates lexical variety and conversational depth.

### 4.3 Three types of comments

**Type 1: Reply on your own post** (critical in the first 30 min)
- Expand + Add a layer when agreeing
- Answer + Bridge when asked
- Acknowledge + Reframe when disagreeing
- Redirect to insight when complimented

**Type 2: Comment on someone else's post** (networking + algorithm training)
- "We did something similar + [result]"
- "One thing I'd add: [insight]"
- "Curious — did you see the same pattern?"
- "Our data shows [different perspective]"

**Type 3: Initiating discussion** (first comment under your own post)
- A bonus insight not in the post
- A directing question
- Behind the scenes

### 4.4 Connection Strategy (calibrated for 360Brew)

**From the courses: Marketing on LinkedIn (Marketing on LinkedIn course, Tier 2), How to Stand Out (Tier 1), How to Build a Strong Professional Brand (How to Build a Strong Brand course, Tier 1)**

**Key shift:** Under 360Brew, quality connections > quantity. The algorithm builds "topic DNA" from interactions — connections from your industry strengthen semantic authority, random connections dilute it. LinkedIn limit: 30,000 connections.

| Action | How | Why |
|----------|-----|-------|
| **Strategic connection requests** | With a personalized note: a specific reference to their post/work, explanation of why | Grow a relevant network without diluting topic DNA |
| **Engage before connecting** | Comment on 2-3 posts before sending the request (15+ words, substantive) | A "warm introduction" — connection is accepted 3x more often |
| **DM conversations** | Not a pitch, but a value-first dialogue. Icebreakers: shared interest, book rec, reaction to their post | Build trust through messaging |
