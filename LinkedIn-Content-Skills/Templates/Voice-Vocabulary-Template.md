# [Author Name] Voice Vocabulary

## IMPORTANT: How to Use This File

**THIS IS NOT A GENERATOR.** Do not grab the first expression you see and stuff it into a post for the sake of "voice".

**Use ONLY when editing drafts** in these cases:

1. **Replacement mode.** The draft has an AI marker or generic phrase → look up what the author says in an equivalent semantic position and swap it in.
2. **Confirmation mode.** You wrote a phrase in the author's style → quickly verify that they actually say it (is there a direct quote in this file).
3. **Lexicon hint on tone loss.** The draft has become too formal / PR-like → look for 1-2 targeted replacements (filler, idiomatic phrase) that bring liveness back.

**WHAT NOT TO DO:**

- Don't insert "characteristic words" into the text where the author hasn't expressed a similar thought in the source material. **Voice ≠ fabrication.**
- Don't pile them in for the sake of quantity. One or two signature turns per post is enough.
- Don't use low-frequency phrases (marked `low`) as a signature — they are one-offs, not a pattern.
- Don't swap English words for Russian ones in EN posts (or vice versa). Code-switching only works in a native context.
- Don't string filler words together in LinkedIn posts the way they appear in live speech — the algorithm penalizes it. Filler works in one or two spots for effect.

**HOW TO CHECK YOURSELF:**

- Every expression you take from this file must match a thought the author actually expressed in the post's source material.
- If the draft has >3 expressions from this file per 1200 characters — that's not humanization anymore, it's pastiche. Roll it back.
