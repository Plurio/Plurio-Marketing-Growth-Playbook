# LinkedIn Posting Time Guidelines

> Rules for optimal posting timing to maximize engagement.
> Data: Buffer (4.8M posts), AuthoredUp (4.2M posts), Sprout Social (2.7B engagements), Hootsuite, LinkBoost, Postiv AI, Richard van der Blom (1.8M posts).
> **Last updated:** 2026-03-28

---

## Best days to post

| Day | Ranking | Comment |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Tuesday** | #1 (tied) | AuthoredUp, Sprout, van der Blom — peak day. Buffer disputes (ranks it lower), but for B2B audiences Tuesday is consistently strong |
| **Wednesday** | #1 (tied) | Buffer (4.8M posts) — #1 day. Professionals return to LinkedIn after a heads-down start of the week |
| **Thursday** | #2 | All sources agree — strong day |
| **Monday** | #3 | Weaker for same-day engagement, BUT: the 48-hour post lifespan captures Tuesday-Wednesday (peak days). Strategically stronger than Friday |
| **Friday** | #4 | Lifespan bleeds into the dead weekend. Only before 14:00 EST and only if time-critical |
| **Saturday-Sunday** | #5-6 | Engagement drops by **40-60%**. Nuance: the top 1% of weekend posts get +25% vs the average weekday post (less competition). But this works for personal/career content, not B2B thought leadership |

**Strategic insight (NEW):** Thanks to the 48-hour lifespan, it's better to pick a day so the post's "tail" lands on strong days:
- **Tuesday** → lifespan covers Wednesday + Thursday = ideal
- **Monday** → lifespan covers Tuesday + Wednesday = good
- **Thursday** → lifespan bleeds into Friday + Saturday = worse
- **Friday** → lifespan bleeds into Saturday + Sunday = bad

**Conclusion:** Tuesday-Wednesday are peak days. Monday is a strategically strong third option. Friday — only as a last resort.

## Optimal time by timezone

Target audience: **USA**
Founder: **Los Angeles (PST)**
Boosters: **Europe (CET)**

### Morning slot (proven)

| LA (PST) | East Coast (EST) | Europe (CET) | Buenos Aires (ART) | Recommendation |
|----------|------------------|--------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **6:00 AM** | 9:00 AM | 15:00 | 11:00 | Ideal: US peak + Europeans at work |
| **7:00 AM** | 10:00 AM | 16:00 | 12:00 | Excellent: US peak hours |
| **8:00 AM** | 11:00 AM | 17:00 | 13:00 | Good for US, Europeans leaving |

### Evening slot (NEW — Buffer 4.8M posts) ⚠️ ONLY for US-only audiences

| LA (PST) | East Coast (EST) | Europe (CET) | Recommendation |
|----------|------------------|--------------|--------------|
| **12:00 PM** | 3:00 PM | 21:00 | US afternoon peak, but Europe is offline |
| **1:00 PM** | 4:00 PM | 22:00 | Strong for US, Europe asleep |
| **2:00 PM** | 5:00 PM | 23:00 | US evening scrolling, Europe asleep |

**New data (Buffer, March 2026):** The engagement peak has shifted from morning hours (9 AM-12 PM) to **3-8 PM EST**. Best slots by day:
- Monday: 5 PM, 9-10 PM EST
- Tuesday: 4-5 PM EST
- Wednesday: 3-5 PM EST
- Thursday: 5 PM, 7-9 PM EST
- Friday: 3-5 PM EST

**⚠️ For our team (audience ~50% Europe / ~50% US):** The evening slot loses the European half of the audience (21:00+ CET). We use it ONLY if: (a) a specific post targets a US-only audience, or (b) European boosters aren't available.

**Our strategy:** The morning slot **6-7 AM LA** remains the primary one — the only window that captures both audiences simultaneously (Europe 15-16:00 + US East 9-10 AM).

### Why 6-7 AM LA still works:
1. European boosters provide early engagement (15-16:00 their time)
2. The US audience sees the post during morning hours (9-10 AM EST)
3. The post builds momentum into the evening peak (3-5 PM EST)

## Golden Window — two-phase model (UPDATED March 2026)

### Phase 1: Initial filter (0-90 minutes)

- The post is shown to **2-5% of your network**
- The algorithm evaluates: dwell time, comment quality, click-through
- **"Great post!" comments = noise**, do not trigger expansion
- Multi-sentence discussions = strong signal to move to Phase 2

### Phase 2: Sustained evaluation (90 min — 8 hours)

- If Phase 1 succeeds → expansion to interest-based clusters (60% reach)
- Evaluated: are comments continuing? Is thread depth growing?
- If yes → broader distribution for 24-48+ hours

### Golden Window tactics

| Action | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Reply to ALL comments within the first **15 min** | **+90% algorithmic boost** |
| Reply within the first 30 min | +64% total comments, 2.3x views |
| Do NOT edit the post | Editing resets the algorithm |
| Comment on 5-10 posts in your niche right after | +20% reach, +55% profile views |
| Notify boosters in advance | Early engagement from relevant people |
| **Reactivation at hour 8 and hour 24 (NEW)** | A comment or self-reshare triggers algorithmic re-evaluation and pushes the post back into feeds |

## Posting frequency (UPDATED March 2026)

| Frequency | Effect | Source |
|---------|--------|----------|
| 1 post/week | Baseline — minimal algorithmic momentum | All |
| **2-3/week (executives)** | **Best ROI** on lead generation for founders and C-level | Van der Blom, LinkBoost |
| **3-5 posts/week** | **Sweet spot** — optimal quality/reach balance | All |
| 5-7/week | Works IF quality is stable. Buffer (4.8M posts): no penalty, impressions grow | Buffer |
| **2+ posts/day** | **PENALTY: -40%+ reach per post.** Content cannibalizes itself | Van der Blom, LinkBoost |

**Key insight:** The real penalty isn't for the number of posts per week, but for **2+ posts in a SINGLE day**. With 1 post/day and stable quality, you can post 5-7 times a week without penalty.

**For our team:**
- **Seva:** 2-3 posts/week — optimal ROI
- **Kirill:** 2-3 posts/week
- **Other authors:** 1-2 posts/week (building momentum)
- **Rule:** minimum 12 hours between posts from the same author, no more than 1 post/day

## Pre-posting checklist

- [ ] Day: Tuesday-Wednesday (ideal), Monday-Thursday (good), Friday before 14:00 EST (last resort)
- [ ] Time: 6-7 AM LA (morning slot) or 12-2 PM LA (evening slot)
- [ ] European boosters are notified and ready to respond in the first 15 minutes
- [ ] The post is not going out on Friday afternoon or over the weekend
- [ ] At least 12 hours since the same author's last post
- [ ] No more than 1 post per day (2+/day = penalty)
- [ ] Reactivation planned: a comment at hour 8 and hour 24

## Worst-case scenarios

| Situation | What to do |
|----------|------------|
| Thursday evening LA time | Better to wait until Tuesday (Thursday's lifespan bleeds into the weekend) |
| Friday morning | Only if highly time-critical, before 14:00 EST. Lifespan vanishes over the weekend |
| Weekend | Don't post B2B thought leadership. Exception: personal/career content can benefit from low competition |
| Monday morning | A good option — lifespan captures Tuesday-Wednesday |
