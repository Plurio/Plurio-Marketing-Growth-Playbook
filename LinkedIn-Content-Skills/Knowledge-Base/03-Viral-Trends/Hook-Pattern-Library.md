# Hook Pattern Library

> A library of proven hook formulas for LinkedIn posts.
> Each hook: formula + adaptation for different authors + real examples.
> **Last updated:** 2026-02-19

---

## How to use

1. Pick a hook formula that fits the content
2. Adapt it to the author's voice (use DIP Speech Patterns)
3. Write 2-3 variants
4. Check: "If I saw this in my feed, would I stop?"

**Rule:** Only the first ~140 characters are visible before "See more". The hook MUST live here.

**360Brew "Lost-in-Distance" Effect (March 2026):** The first sentence gets **disproportionate attention weighting** from the 360Brew algorithm. A vague or generic prologue → deprioritization **BEFORE** people scroll. That means the first line is the anchor not only for the reader but for the algorithm itself. The semantic anchor determines how 360Brew interprets and ranks the ENTIRE post.

**Formatting:** First line → double line break → second line. This gives you control over what's visible before "See more".

## FORMULA 1: Contrarian Take

**Formula:** "Everyone thinks [X]. They're wrong." / "The popular advice about [X] is dead wrong."

**When to use:** The author holds a position that contradicts the mainstream. There's data or experience to back it up.

**Examples:**

| Example | Author Style | Industry |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| "AI-first doesn't mean using ChatGPT. Here's what it actually means." | Seva — visionary, redefining terms | AI/Tech |
| "I stopped opening dashboards 3 weeks ago. Best decision this quarter." | Kirill — practical, personal proof | Marketing |
| "Everyone talks about scaling ads. Nobody talks about when NOT to scale." | Performance — contrarian caution | Marketing |

**Danger zone:** Contrarianism for its own sake = empty. You need real evidence or personal experience.

## FORMULA 2: Personal Story / Vulnerability

**Formula:** "I [did something / lost something / failed at something]. Here's what happened." / "Last [time period], [something unexpected happened]."

**When to use:** There's a real story in the DIP Stories Bank. The story leads to an actionable lesson.

**Examples:**

| Example | Author Style | Industry |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| "I built a 100-person agency. Then I left it all to build something new." | Seva — founder journey | Business |
| "Two startups. A four-year-old daughter. And a move to Lisbon." | Kirill — personal + founder | Founder |
| "I was afraid our product wasn't ready. We launched anyway." | Founder — vulnerability + lesson | Startup |

**Rule:** 8.5x average engagement for authentic vulnerability. But it MUST be genuine — manufactured vulnerability = backlash.

## FORMULA 3: Quantified Outcome / Data Shock

**Formula:** "How I achieved [specific result] in [time] with [method]." / "[X]% of [audience] do [thing]. They get [Y]% of results."

**When to use:** You have concrete numbers from real experience. Numbers stop scroll.

**Examples:**

| Example | Author Style | Industry |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| "We cut $3.45 per creative in testing costs. One agent did it." | Kirill — specific numbers | Marketing/AI |
| "30+ people. One workspace. Zero meetings about who edits what." | Seva — operational proof | AI-first ops |
| "Only 3% of marketers track LTV beyond day 30. They outperform by 4x." | Data-driven — surprising stat | Performance |

**Danger zone:** Fake or inflated numbers = trust destruction. Always use real data from the DIP or Benchmarks.

## FORMULA 4: Question-Led

**Formula:** "Are you making this mistake with [X]?" / "What would you do if [specific scenario]?"

**When to use:** The topic is relevant to a broad audience. The question prompts self-reflection.

**Examples:**

| Example | Author Style | Industry |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| "What if your dashboards could talk back to you?" | Product-visionary | AI/Analytics |
| "Are you optimizing for the wrong metrics?" | Challenging assumptions | Performance |
| "How many hours a week do you spend on reports nobody reads?" | Pain point — relatable | Marketing ops |

**Rule:** The question must be specific, not generic. "What do you think?" = engagement bait. "What would you do if your top campaign stopped converting at 3 AM?" = genuine.

## FORMULA 5: Authoritative / Experience-Based

**Formula:** "After [N years / N clients / N experiments], I learned [N things]." / "I've [done X] for [N years]. Here's what nobody tells you."

**When to use:** The author has deep experience in the topic. Credibility through track record.

**Examples:**

| Example | Author Style | Industry |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| "20 years in performance marketing. 5 things I'd do differently." | Seva — veteran authority | Marketing |
| "I've managed $100K+/month ad budgets for 15+ clients. Here's the pattern." | Kirill — practitioner proof | Performance |

---

## FORMULA 6: Problem & Agitation

**Formula:** "You work [X hours] but see [no results]." / "[Pain point] is killing your [outcome]."

**When to use:** Targeting a known pain point. Post offers a solution.

**Examples:**

| Example | Author Style | Industry |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| "Your attribution data is lying to you. And it's costing you thousands." | Problem → product angle | Attribution |
| "Marketers are afraid to scale. Here's why that fear is rational." | Kirill — empathetic + honest | Performance |
| "You're spending 40% of your time on tasks AI could do in seconds." | Relatable pain → AI solution | AI/Productivity |

---

## FORMULA 7: Behind-the-Scenes / Build in Public

**Formula:** "Here's what [X] actually looks like behind the curtain." / "We just [built/launched/broke X]. Here's the real story."

**When to use:** Sharing internal process, decisions, failures. Authenticity > polish.

**Examples:**

| Example | Author Style | Industry |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| "We ship features every day. Here's what our workflow actually looks like." | Seva — AI-first ops transparency | Startup ops |
| "This is what our AI agent's reasoning chain looks like. Screenshot inside." | Kirill — product demo | AI Product |

**Trending:** "Build in public" = growing trend in 2026. High authenticity signal.

## ANTI-HOOKS (what to NEVER use)

| Anti-Hook | Why It Fails |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Some thoughts on..." | Zero curiosity. Will be scrolled past |
| "I wanted to share..." | Weak opener. No hook energy |
| "In this post I will..." | Kills intrigue. Academic, not conversational |
| "Exciting news!" | Overused. Algorithm flags as promotional |
| "Happy Monday!" / "TGIF!" | Generic. No value signal |
| "Agree?" (as the hook) | Engagement bait — penalized |
| Very long first sentence | Loses reader before "See more" |
| Starting with a hashtag | Looks spammy |

## Hook Testing Checklist

- [ ] Is this specific (not generic)?
- [ ] Does it create curiosity or tension?
- [ ] Would I stop scrolling for this?
- [ ] Does it sound like the author (check DIP)?
- [ ] Is it under 140 characters (fits before "See more")?
- [ ] Does the body deliver on the hook's promise?
