### Pattern 3: What HIGH-follower profiles do differently

**100K+ followers (6 profiles):**
- 3 out of 6 have NO company role visible in first 60 chars (Lenny, Kyle, Katelyn)
- 2 are ultra-minimal (Elena 17 chars, Katelyn 20 chars)
- Average headline length: **49 chars** — much shorter than the 220 max
- Focus: WHAT they offer, not WHO they are

**Under 10K followers (10 profiles):**
- 9 out of 10 lead with "CEO/Founder/Co-Founder at [Company]"
- Average headline length: **46 chars** — also short, but filled with role + company only
- Focus: WHO they are, not WHAT they offer
- Almost zero value proposition or differentiation

**Insight:** Low-follower profiles are short AND empty (just role). High-follower profiles are short BUT intentional (value or bold statement). The difference isn't length — it's whether the headline answers "why should I care?"

## TWO STRATEGIES EMERGE

### Strategy A: "The Lenny" — Value-First, No Role

Lead with what you offer, not who you are. Works for: established personal brands, newsletter/content-driven.

```
Examples:
- "Deeply researched no-nonsense product, growth, and career advice" (Lenny, 352K)
- "Growth Unhinged | Real-life growth insights, playbooks, and case studies" (Kyle, 106K)
- "Idea owners get rich" (Katelyn, 94K)
```

**Pro:** Maximum differentiation. Memorable. Signals thought leadership.
**Con:** Requires existing brand recognition. No company discovery.

### Strategy B: "The Builder" — Role + Value Prop + Proof

Lead with role, then explain what you do and why it matters.

```
Examples:
- "CEO @ Retention.com & RB2B | Person-Level Website Visitor Identity | 70-80% of Your Website Traffic" (Adam, 151K)
- "Martech Analyst & Advisor | Ex-HubSpot VP Platform Ecosystem | 'Godfather of Martech' – AdAge" (Scott, 57K)
- "CEO @ Disruptive Digital, a Top Meta Agency Partner | Ex-Facebook" (Maurice, 13K)
```

**Pro:** Clear positioning. Company gets visibility. Keywords for search.
**Con:** More generic feel. Needs strong differentiator to stand out.

### Strategy C: "The Ultra-Minimal" — Extreme simplicity

Just role + company. Let the content speak.

```
Examples:
- "Growth at Lovable" (Elena, 203K)
- "CEO at Jones Road Beauty" (Cody, 12K)
```

**Pro:** Clean, confident. Works when company is well-known or content is very strong.
**Con:** Zero information for new visitors. No differentiation.
