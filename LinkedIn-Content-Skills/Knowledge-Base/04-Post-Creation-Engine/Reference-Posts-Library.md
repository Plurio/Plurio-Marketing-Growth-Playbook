# Reference Posts Library

> A collection of external posts/newsletters that demonstrate strong content framing frameworks.
> Used as a **reference for format and structure**, not as a source of facts.

---

## How to use

1. When creating a post, Claude asks: "Want to anchor on one of the references?"
2. If yes — the author picks a reference from the list below
3. Claude adapts the reference's **structure and device** to the author's topic and DIP
4. Content and facts come ONLY from the author's canonical sources (transcripts, DIP Stories Bank)

**Important:** a reference = inspiration for format/structure, NOT copying the text.

### REF-001: Value-First Product Story (Seedance 2.0 / The Social Signal)

**Source:** The Social Signal newsletter, Iris Tu (Abstack), Feb 23 2026
**Original topic:** Seedance 2.0 — how an AI video tool is changing marketing

**Framework: "The world has shifted — here's what's now possible"**

| Element | How it works | Example from the original |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Hook** | A statement about an industry shift | "Your X feed is probably full of Seedance 2 videos right now" |
| **Before/After** | Concrete comparison of workflow before and after | Before: 7 steps, manual editing. After: 4 steps, 80% time saved |
| **How it works** | Mechanics explained without jargon | Agentic system, base model strength, multimodal reference |
| **Impact by audience** | Value broken down by segment | Marketing Impact, Consumer Impact — separate blocks |
| **Visual proof** | Screenshots/examples of results | Ad samples "created in 10 minutes", TVC style vs UGC style |
| **Honest limitations** | Doesn't hide constraints → builds trust | "Security constraints are evolving daily", "not perfect" |
| **Forward-looking close** | A perspective, not a sales pitch | "The question is how quickly you adapt" |

**Which of our posts this fits:**
- Telling the story of Plurio Chat / the AI agent through a concrete use case
- Demonstrating a workflow "before Plurio / after Plurio"
- New product features framed as "the world has shifted"
- A client case: how a specific task is now solved in a new way

**How to adapt for Plurio:**
1. Open with a concrete shift in performance marketing (creative fatigue, LTV delays, manual rules)
2. Show Before/After for a specific workflow (e.g., manually pausing campaigns vs auto-rules)
3. Explain the mechanics: what the AI agent does, how it works
4. Break value down by role: performance marketer, CMO, analyst
5. Show honest limitations ("it's not a magic button")
6. Close with a perspective, not a demo CTA

### REF-002: Off-Topic Expertise Story + Industry Doom

**Source:** An external LinkedIn author (anonymized), March 2026
**Original topic:** Crisis in the gaming industry — successful games no longer save teams from layoffs
**Performance:** 33,000+ impressions, 60+ comments (organically, with no pumped-up account)
**Author profile:** Building a B2B SaaS tool for writing LinkedIn content. The post is NOT about their product.

**Framework: "I was inside another industry — here's what I saw, and it concerns everyone"**

| Element | How it works | Example from the original |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Hook — paradox** | Opens with a contradiction your head can't reconcile. Not a question, not clickbait — a fact | "You can make a hit game, sell 7 million copies in three days, and still watch your team get laid off." |
| **Celebrity anchor** | Quotes well-known industry figures → credibility + SEO effect. A photo with them = scroll-stop | John and Brenda Romero, BAFTA photo. "Definitely crashier" — direct quote |
| **Personal authority claim** | One paragraph establishing the right to speak on the topic. Not bragging — observation | "I spent 1.5 years inside the gaming industry. From the outside... In reality..." |
| **Data points** | Specific numbers, not roundings. Every figure = a proof point | "$300M+ budgets", "6 million+ sales to break even", "110 staff to 9" |
| **Emotional pivot** | After the data — a line that lands on a human level. Not sentimentality — a hard truth | "Success doesn't protect you anymore. Hit or flop, you end up in the same place." |
| **Second thread (AI angle)** | Introduces a parallel topic that raises the stakes. Not the main topic, but an amplifier | "AI is quietly entering through the back door... 85% of gamers say they're against AI" |
| **Open-ended close** | NOT "here's what I think" — but "I genuinely don't know". Invites thought, not argument | "I genuinely don't know how this resolves... What's your read on where this goes?" |
| **Photo = social proof** | Photo of the author with well-known people at an event. Not a stock photo — a real moment | BAFTA Games Awards red carpet photo with John & Brenda Romero |

**Why it took off (analysis):**
1. **Off-topic expertise** — the post is not about the author's product but about an industry they have been inside. LinkedIn rewards breadth (not just your niche)
2. **Photo with well-known people** — scroll-stop + credibility + "who is that?" effect
3. **Paradox in the hook** — "7M copies and layoffs" — the brain wants to resolve the contradiction
4. **Open ending** — "I don't know" instead of an expert verdict → comments, because people have something to say
5. **Two layers of theme** (gaming crisis + AI) — two different audience segments react
6. **Dwell time** — long text with specifics, read all the way through

**Which of our posts this fits:**
- The author talks about an industry from behind the scenes (gaming, fintech, EdTech — anything they have seen through clients)
- "I was inside [industry], here's what I saw" cases — any anonymized client vertical
- Posts about AI displacement / future of work through the lens of a specific industry
- Any post with a paradox (success + failure simultaneously)

**How to adapt:**
1. Pick an industry the author has genuinely worked in
2. Open with a paradox from that industry (specific numbers: "spend $X, lose Y")
3. Establish personal authority: "I spent [time] working with [N] companies in [industry]"
4. Provide 3–4 specific data points (budgets, results, layoffs, growth)
5. Emotional pivot: what follows for people, not for business
6. Optional: a second thread (AI/automation angle — our expertise)
7. Close with a question, not a verdict
8. Photo: if there is a photo with a well-known person from the industry — use it

**Full post text (for reference):**

> You can make a hit game, sell 7 million copies in three days, and still watch your team get laid off.
>
> That's what happened with EA's Battlefield. And John and Brenda Romero - people who lived through the 1983 crash - say what's happening now is worse. "Definitely crashier" is how Brenda put it.
>
> I spent 1.5 years inside the gaming industry. From the outside, it looks like creativity and cool ideas. In reality, it's one of the most data-driven industries out there - these companies understand economics deeply and work with it seriously. Which makes the current situation harder to explain, not easier.
>
> AAA budgets now run $300M+. You need 6 million+ sales just to break even. When that math doesn't work, projects get cancelled mid-development, studios shut down, and people who've worked together for a decade get cut. Romero Games went from 110 staff to 9.
>
> But the part that's genuinely hard to process: success doesn't protect you anymore. Hit or flop, you end up in the same place.
>
> And AI is quietly entering through the back door while all this is happening. 85% of gamers say they're against AI in games. Developers push back too. Publishers push forward anyway - because the economics, apparently, demand it.
>
> I genuinely don't know how this resolves. The people who've seen a crash before are saying this one feels different - not because people will stop playing games, but because the industry is cutting the people who make them while simultaneously ignoring the audience those people are trying to serve.
>
> What's your read on where this goes?

**Visual:** Photo of John & Brenda Romero at BAFTA Games Awards (red carpet, casual/authentic).

### REF-003: Build-In-Public SaaS Replacement + Event Funnel (VC Founder, LinkedIn)

**Source:** VC Founder (vibecoding community), LinkedIn, April 2026
**Original topic:** Replaced Notion + Airtable + Google Drive with a vibecoded solution in 1 month
**Performance:** 273+ reactions, 647 comments, 4 reposts
**Author profile:** VC, AI community leader. The post = build-in-public + event lead-gen

**Framework: "We built it ourselves — come see how"**

| Element | How it works | Example from the original |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Hook — accomplishment** | Short "We did it." — a fact, not a promise | "We did it. Exactly 1 month ago we decided to move..." |
| **Skepticism → proof** | The author was the skeptic → relatable arc | "I was skeptical when our 20-year dev suggested we can do it" |
| **Competitive framing** | Direct comparison to known products | "Works much faster than Notion, more flexible than Airtable" |
| **Nuanced take** | Not "X is dead" — a mature stance | "Not the companies though. Great companies, amazing founders" |
| **Event CTA + scarcity** | Event + social proof (hundreds signed up) | "On April 15th... several hundred people signed up" |
| **"Plus" CTA** | Minimal barrier → mass comments | "Put a plus if you'd like an invite link" → 647 comments |
| **Meme-visual** | AI illustration: kid coder vs panicked SaaS mascots | Kid with laptop + animals in Notion/Drive/Airtable shirts running away |

**Why it took off:**
1. **"Put a plus" CTA** — barrier = 0, every "+" = a comment → algorithm. 647 comments
2. **Hot topic** — vibecoding + SaaS replacement triggers both skeptics and enthusiasts
3. **Event funnel** — the post = lead-gen. Reach + registrations at the same time
4. **Nuanced opinion** — not "SaaS is dead" → trust, fewer haters
5. **Meme-visual** — custom 3D with competitor logos = scroll-stop + shareability

**For our posts:**
- Seva: AI-first workspace, "we replaced [tool] with an agent"
- Webinar/demo announcement through a build-in-public story
- Kirill: "how we rebuilt the workflow" through Plurio
- Any post with a concrete vibecoding result

**Full post text:**

> We did it. Exactly 1 month ago we decided to move from Notion + Airtable + Google Drive to our homegrown (vibecoded) solution. Today, we made the move.
>
> I was skeptical when our 20-year dev suggested we can do it. It is one thing to vibecode a dashboard, it is another to build something architecturally complex, that supports async collaboration and needs to work fast, even though it is an internal tool.
>
> At this point, we have gone beyond what Notion, Airtable, and Drive have to offer.
>
> Our Brain (internal name) works much faster than Notion and is more flexible than Airtable. We have built extensible software where any team member can build on top of any functionality using shared credentials and data infrastructure. We have an internal marketplace of agents and skills and an almost Lovable-like experience for extending and customizing the Brain. We built what I believe what the future of software is about.
>
> I am at the forefront of all the tech, playing with almost every significant tool, vibecoding, inside a bunch of super active AI communities with the smartest people in the world. But building this hands on has still been eye opening. I do not think Notion, Airtable, Monday are dead. But I do think the generic assumption that SaaS will stay because enterprises or prosumers will not vibecode is very wrong. The current versions of these products are doomed in my opinion. Not the companies though. These are great companies, run by amazing founders, who have done this once and will find a way to reinvent themselves.
>
> On April 15th, I will be sharing for our community why we did it, what we built, and doing a live demo.
>
> The call will be useful for CEOs to raise the bar for what they expect from their teams, as well as for VCs and LPs to learn how a fellow VC operates. Our secret sauce is not tech, but tech helps us dedicate more time to the secret sauce. Everybody is welcome subject to broadcasting limits.
>
> My presentation will not take more than 15 minutes. I am looking for 3 more people to demo products they've built to replace their SaaS stack. Write in comments if you'd like to present. There are already several hundred people signed up. This promises to be a big call.
>
> Put a plus if you'd like an invite link.

## When NOT to use a reference

- When the post is a hot take or a personal story (a reference formalizes it, but spontaneity is needed)
- When the author already knows the exact format
- When the topic doesn't fit any framework (better to write "from scratch")
