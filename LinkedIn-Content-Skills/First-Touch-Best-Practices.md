# LinkedIn First Touch Message - Best Practices

> **Purpose**: Guidelines for writing the first message after LinkedIn connection is established. This is "Touch 1" in your outreach sequence — building rapport before any ask.

**Last Updated**: 2026-01-27
**Use Case**: First message to experts/founders after they accept your connection request
**Related**: See [LinkedIn-Connection-Notes-Best-Practices.md](LinkedIn-Connection-Notes-Best-Practices.md) for connection request notes

---

## Context: What is Touch 1?

**Touch 1** is the first real message after connection is established. Unlike the connection note (which was 2-3 sentences), this can be longer and more detailed.

**Goal**: Build genuine rapport, show you know their work, create a foundation for future collaboration. No pitch, no ask (or very light ask).

**Timing**: Send 1-3 days after connection accepted, or immediately if already connected.

---

## Core Philosophy

This is a **founder-to-founder conversation**, not a sales pitch. You're reaching out because you genuinely follow their work and want to start a relationship. The message should feel like it's from a peer, not a vendor.

---

## ❌ What NEVER Works

### Generic Structure
- ❌ Starting with "I hope this message finds you well"
- ❌ "I'd love to pick your brain"
- ❌ "Can I ask you some questions about..."
- ❌ "I'm reaching out because..." (sounds like sales)
- ❌ Long paragraphs about your company/product
- ❌ Immediate pitch or invitation

### Generic Openers
- ❌ "Thanks for connecting!"
- ❌ "I've been impressed by your work"
- ❌ "I noticed we both work in [industry]"
- ❌ "I'd love to learn more about [their company]"

### Why These Fail
They sound templated, sales-y, or like you haven't actually followed their work.

---

## ✅ What Actually Works

### 1. Start With Specific Content/Work

**Good examples:**
- "Your Hightide 120-day experiment caught my eye"
- "The org chart you posted showing your 40+ AI agents at Relay"
- "Your posts on modular video ad frameworks keep showing up in my feed"
- "Been following The Agile Brand for a while — congrats on the 800-episode milestone"

**Why it works:** Proves you actually know their work, not just their LinkedIn headline.

---

### 2. Show Deep Understanding (Not Surface Praise)

Don't just say "great post" — explain WHY it caught your attention or what problem it solves:

**Good examples:**
- "That's serious consistency in a space where most podcasts fade after 50"
- "Having AI make every decision for an eCommerce brand — that's the kind of test most people theorize about but never actually run"
- "The breakdown of hooks, visuals, CTAs as testable units — it's the kind of systematic thinking most creative teams skip"
- "The 70% CTR prediction accuracy is particularly interesting. Proves ad performance isn't the black box everyone pretends it is"

**Why it works:** Shows domain expertise and that you understand the implications of their work.

---

### 3. Reference Multiple Pieces of Their Work

**Good approach:**
- First paragraph: Specific content piece #1
- Second paragraph: What makes it interesting / related work
- Third paragraph: Additional content piece or angle (optional)
- Fourth paragraph: Light closer or no closer at all

**Example (Manson Chen):**
```
Your posts on modular video ad frameworks keep showing up in my feed for good reason. The breakdown of hooks, visuals, CTAs as testable units — it's the kind of systematic thinking most creative teams skip.

Curious about Sovran — the idea of remixing and launching ads instantly vs. the traditional brief-to-production cycle sounds like it solves the bottleneck most performance teams hit.

Also appreciated your piece on growth team compensation. The burnout angle doesn't get talked about enough.

Would enjoy connecting sometime.
```

---

### 4. Ask Thoughtful Questions (Optional)

**If you ask questions, make them:**
- About their expertise (not asking for help)
- Showing genuine curiosity
- Demonstrating you understand the complexity

**Good examples:**
- "I'm curious about the governance side: how do you manage trust/hallucinations across 40 agents?"
- "Is there a review layer, or have you built enough guardrails that it's self-correcting?"

**Bad examples:**
- "Can you explain how your product works?"
- "Would love to learn from you"
- "Any tips for someone starting out?"

---

### 5. Light Closer (or No Closer)

**Best options:**
1. **Just respect/appreciation** — "Just wanted to say — respect the work."
2. **Very light interest** — "Would enjoy connecting sometime."
3. **Question left open** — "Would love to hear your thinking on [specific topic]."
4. **No closer at all** — end after showing appreciation

**Never:**
- "Let me know if you'd be open to a call"
- "Would love to explore opportunities to collaborate"
- "Can I ask you a few questions?"

---

## Structure & Length

### Ideal Structure
```
[Subject line - if email/InMail]

[Paragraph 1: Specific content you saw + why it caught your attention]

[Paragraph 2: Deeper dive on why it matters / what problem it solves]

[Paragraph 3: Additional reference to their work OR thoughtful question (optional)]

[Paragraph 4: Light closer or appreciation (optional)]

[Your name]
```

### Length
- **3-4 paragraphs** (unlike connection note which is 2-3 sentences)
- **150-300 words** ideal
- **Can be longer** if you're going deep on their work, but keep focused

## What To Mention About Yourself (If At All)

**Rule:** Mention yourself minimally or not at all in Touch 1.

**If you must:**
- One sentence max
- Focus on what you're building, not selling
- No product features

**Good:**
- "Building AI agents for performance marketing." (if relevant to their work)
- "Working on [specific problem] for [audience]."

**Bad:**
- Detailed description of your product
- List of features
- Pitch for what you do

**Best:** Don't mention yourself at all. Make it 100% about them.

## Subject Lines (If Email/InMail)

**Formula:** [Specific content/achievement] — [your angle]

**Good examples:**
- "Your 800-episode milestone + Change Agents series"
- "The Hightide experiment — fascinating approach"
- "Modular creative testing — your Cash App/Calm learnings"
- "Your 40-agent org chart — questions"

**Bad examples:**
- "Quick question"
- "Following up"
- "Interested in connecting"
- "Great content!"

## Timing & Context

### When To Send Touch 1
- **After connection accepted**: 1-3 days after they accept
- **Already connected**: Can send anytime, but ideally after engaging with 2-3 of their posts first

### Before Sending
- [ ] Like 2-3 of their recent posts
- [ ] Read their last 5-10 posts to find specific content to reference
- [ ] Check if they have any major recent announcements/milestones
- [ ] Understand what problem they're solving or what they're known for

---

## Red Flags - Edit If You See These

If your message includes ANY of these, rewrite:
- ❌ "I hope this finds you well"
- ❌ "Thanks for connecting!"
- ❌ "I'd love to pick your brain"
- ❌ "Can I ask you..."
- ❌ "I'm reaching out because..."
- ❌ More than one paragraph about your company
- ❌ Immediate invitation to call/meet
- ❌ Generic praise without specifics
- ❌ Copy-paste template feel

## Real-World Test

Before sending, ask yourself:
1. Did I reference specific content I actually saw? → **If no, rewrite**
2. Does this sound like a sales pitch? → **If yes, rewrite**
3. Could this message be sent to 20 other people with name swaps? → **If yes, rewrite**
4. Am I asking for something in this first message? → **If yes, remove it**
5. Would I respond to this message if I received it? → **If no, rewrite**
